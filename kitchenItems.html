Murg Musallam
Murgh musallam (Masala roasted chicken) is a Mughlai dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It consists of a chicken marinated in a ginger-garlic paste, stuffed with boiled eggs and seasoned with spices l ike saffron, cinnamon, cloves, poppy seeds, cardamom and chilli. It is cooked dry or in sauce, and decorated with almonds and silver leaves.
Murgh musallam literally means 'whole chicken'. The dish was popular among the royal Mughal families of Awadh, now the state of Uttar Pradesh in India. It also means well done.

Chelow Kabab
Chelow Kebab is an Iranian dish consisting of cooked rice (čelow) and one of the many varieties of Iranian kebab. It is considered as the "national dish" of Iran, and was probably created by the time of the Qajar dynasty.
Chelow Kabab has gained its popularity among the foodies of Kolkata since last few decades and became one of the few foreign dishes that has become a household name.
Chelow Kebab is served with accompaniments such as butter, sumac powder, basil, onions, grilled tomatoes, and egg yolk. The traditional beverage accompanied with chelow kabab is doogh, an Iranian yogurt-based drink, sometimes made of carbonated water.
In the old bazaar tradition, the rice and accompaniments are served first, immediately followed by the kebabs, which are threaded on skewers, as well as a piece of flat bread (typically lavash). A skewer is placed directly on the rice and while holding the kabab down on the rice with the bread, the skewer is quickly pulled out.

Rabri
Rabri is a sweet, condensed-milk-based dish, originating from the Indian subcontinent, made by boiling the milk on low heat for a long time until it becomes dense and changes its color to pinkish. Sugar, spices and nuts are added to it to give it flavor. It is chilled and served as dessert. Rabri is the main ingredient in several desserts, such as rasabali, chhena kheeri, and khira sagara.
This very rich dessert is made by heating sweetened milk in a large open vessel (kadhai). As the layer of cream begins to form on the surface of the milk, it is taken off and kept aside. The process continues until the milk is exhausted. The rabri thus formed is extremely creamy and very heavy on the stomach, but loved by the average Bengali.

Aam Patishapta
Mango is the seasonal fruit of summer whereas Patishapta is a delicacy of winter. Aam Patishapta is a fusion of summer and winter to serve the appetite.
Patishapta is a popular Bengali dessert. These are thin crepes made with flour, rice flour among the primary ingredients. Stuffing can be done with processed coconut or khir/kheer.
Processed coconut is prepared by pouring shredded coconut in hot kadai (pan), adding milk and sugar. You can add elaichi (cardamom) to enrich the flavor.
Malpoa
Malpoa is one of the most popular sweet item of Bengal and Odisha. It is also categorized as a 'pitha' that is prepared on the last day of winter month 'Poush' which is also called 'Poush Sankranti' or Makar Sankranti.
There are different varieties of malpoa where the ingredients vary such as banana, mango, coconut etc. But the primary ingredients common to most of the varieties are flour and milk.
You can use serving spoons to pour the batter in a 'kadai' full of hot oil to make the fried hand-palm sized malpoa. Then dip the piece into sugar syrup and serve it.
Ice Cream Sondesh
One of the rare delicacies of Bengal. Bengal is already famous for its special brand called 'Sondesh' or 'Sandesh'. Icecream Sondesh is one of the not-so-common model of that brand.
Though you can make it from cottage-cheese, but to get the best of it, you will need 'chana' or 'chena'. You can make chana by dropping few drops of lime in pre-boiling milk and then rinsing the water off the mound. Add flavors, sugar as per your requirement and you get this awesome sweet.
